Sacramento

Sacramento, the capital city of California, is a vibrant urban center situated in the fertile Central Valley of the state. Home to a diverse population, Sacramento is a microcosm of California's cultural richness. Founded in 1848, during the throes of the Gold Rush, Sacramento quickly transformed from a modest settlement into a bustling city. California joined the Union on September 9, 1850, becoming the 31st state of the United States. Positioned in the western region of the United States, Sacramento serves as the political and cultural heart of California, offering a gateway to both natural beauty and economic opportunity. The city's average income level hovers around $65,000, showcasing a blend of socioeconomic backgrounds. Classified as an urban area, Sacramento boasts a dynamic landscape, with a mix of historic neighborhoods, modern districts, and cultural landmarks that together create a unique and inviting urban experience.
