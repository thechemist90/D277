Los Angeles

Los Angeles, the second-largest city in the United States, is a sprawling urban metropolis nestled in Southern California. Boasting a diverse population and a captivating blend of cultures, it has become an iconic global hub for entertainment, technology, and innovation. Established as a Spanish pueblo in 1781, Los Angeles flourished into a major city over the years and is now a centerpiece of the state. California joined the Union on September 9, 1850, as the 31st state of the United States. Situated in the western region of the country, Los Angeles enjoys a Mediterranean climate and an enviable coastal location. The city's average income level varies significantly, with median household incomes around $62,000, reflecting the diverse economic landscape of the region. Classified as an urban center, Los Angeles is characterized by its vibrant neighborhoods, cultural landmarks, and bustling city life, making it a captivating blend of modernity and history.


Fun Facts

  1. The population of Los Angeles in 2023 was estimated to be 3,769,485.
  2. The city is the second most populous city in the United States, after New York City.
  3. The average income in Los Angeles is $30,225, compared to the state average income is $33,719
