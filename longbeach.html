Long Beach

Long Beach, a coastal city in Southern California, holds a distinctive charm as a diverse and lively urban destination. Home to a population with a rich tapestry of backgrounds, Long Beach has evolved into a unique cultural hub. Its development began as a seaside resort in the late 19th century, and it has grown to become a significant port city and thriving community. California was admitted to the Union on September 9, 1850, becoming the 31st state of the United States. Long Beach is situated within the larger Southern California region, offering a pleasant Mediterranean climate and a picturesque waterfront location. The city's average income level mirrors its varied demographics, with median household incomes approximating $60,000. Classified as an urban area, Long Beach showcases a blend of historic neighborhoods and contemporary urban development, presenting a dynamic and engaging environment for its residents and visitors alike.


Fun Facts
